IMRAN AHMED KHAN NIAZI

The politician and former cricketer

Here is a timeline of Imran Khan's life;

  • 1952 - Khan was born in Lahore
  • 1971 - Imran Khan makes his international cricket debut for Pakistan against England.
  • 1976 - Khan achieves his first Test century against New Zealand.
  • 1979 - Imran Khan leads Pakistan to their first-ever Test series victory in England.
  • 1982 - Khan is appointed as the captain of the Pakistan national cricket team.
  • 1992 - Imran Khan leads Pakistan to their first Cricket World Cup victory, defeating England in the final.
  • 1992 - Khan announces his retirement from international cricket after the World Cup triumph.
  • 1996 - Imran Khan establishes the Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI) political party.
  • 2002 - PTI participates in the general elections but fails to win any seats.
  • 2013 - PTI emerges as the second-largest party in the general elections, and Imran Khan becomes the opposition leader in the National Assembly.
  • 2018 - PTI wins the general elections, and Imran Khan becomes the 22nd Prime Minister of Pakistan.
  • 2020 - Khan faces various challenges during his tenure as Prime Minister, including economic issues, foreign policy challenges, and the COVID-19 pandemic.
  • 2021 - Imran Khan continues to implement his vision for a "Naya Pakistan" (New Pakistan), focusing on anti-corruption measures, social welfare programs, and regional peace initiatives.
  • 2023 - Imran Khan remains the Prime Minister of Pakistan, working towards his goals of economic development, poverty alleviation, and improving governance.
